The Problem

Students want to learn new skills - but face three key barriers

The issue isn’t lack of knowledge -
it’s lack of structure and accessibility

01

Skill Learning is often expensive

Students have limited access to affordable learning options

02

Void of Peer-to-Peer & Community-driven Learning

Most existing platforms are build top-down

03
Student knowledge remains unused

There is no system for students to share their knowledge.

Business Model

To ensure long-term sustainability, we designed a hybrid business model

Credit - System

The hardest problem was designing a system that feels:
fair, flexible & hard to exploit

The Solution: A credit-based System -
Replacing money with trust & contribution

01
Credit Value

03

The Solution

SkillSwap is a credit-based platform where students exchange skills.

How it works:
Teach a skill → earn credits
Spend credits → learn a skill

The Target Users

University students -  as both learners and teachers

Diverse Skillset

universities automatically offer a wide variety of academical skills

Comfort Level IT

ability to have videocalls + use the app without requiring (too much) additional help

Limited Budget

often similar limited income => paid learning is less accessible

Culture & Language

already very international community, similar English Skills

Motivation

help with classes / career,
productive free time,
build connections

Literature Research

Validated through research: Learning is shifting, but tools haven’t caught up.

85 %

of college students consider extra-curricular skills as crucial for a successful career

87 %

of executives and workers report skill gaps

63 %

of workers believe they need continuous training to keep up with the  job market

58 %

of workers want to learn new skills

72 %

prefer collaborative learning over traditional approaches

Only 30 %

of workers have access to affordable training

MVP

The key features of our final product

Skill exchange

offers opportunity to search and filter for skills a users needs or wants to learn
creates best peer to peer matches for each search
allows users to add their own skills available to other users as teaching or service

Community projects

Offers the opportunity to connect as a group while distributing different skills
Users can join existing projects using their own skills or create their own for others to partcipate in

Profile

created during Signup process => regulary updated
including own Skills & Projects, Credits,              Feedback from other users & Rating Results
visible to all other users
