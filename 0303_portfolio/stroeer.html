Overview

Out-of-home media at
national scale.

Ströer SE & Co. KGaA is one of Germany's largest out-of-home advertising companies, operating digital public displays across high-traffic locations nationwide — from train stations and airports to city centres and shopping malls.

As a working student in the visual and motion design team, I contributed to creating content for these screens — designing for the unique constraints of public display environments where attention is fleeting and visual impact must be instant.

NDA applies. All client campaigns, brand assets, and specific design work produced during this role are covered by a non-disclosure agreement. Details, visuals, and case specifics cannot be shared publicly.

My Role
  • Visual design for digital out-of-home (DOOH) campaigns
  • Motion design and animated content for public displays
  • Adapting brand and campaign assets to screen formats
  • Working within tight display constraints — short view windows, high ambient noise
  • Collaboration with the content and advertising teams
Design Context

Designing for public attention.

DOOH content has unique design constraints. Audiences are in motion, exposure is measured in seconds, and screens compete with high-stimulus environments. Effective design here is about visual hierarchy, immediate legibility, and motion that guides rather than distracts.

Visual Design

Creating static and animated layouts for digital screens — balancing brand guidelines with the practical demands of high-ambient environments and diverse display sizes.

Motion Design

Designing motion sequences that communicate clearly within 5–15 second loops — using animation to direct attention, reveal information progressively, and maintain legibility in motion.

Format Adaptation

Adapting campaign materials across a range of display formats — from portrait city-light panels to large-format landscape boards — maintaining visual consistency at every scale.

Design Insight

Public display design is as much about what you leave out as what you put in. The best-performing content is radically simple — one message, one visual anchor, one clear moment of communication. Complexity is lost to the environment.
