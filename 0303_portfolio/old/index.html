About Me.

I’m a UX/UI and Web/App Designer with a strong background in Media Informatics, combining thoughtful design with a solid understanding of technology. My work focuses on creating detailed usable, engaging and inclusive interfaces that connect people across contexts, cultures, and abilities, stepby step.

Whether collaborating in interdisciplinary teams or working independently, I value structured thinking, open communication, and design decisions grounded in user needs. I enjoy turning complex ideas into intuitive experiences. I’m always curious to explore new tools, methods, and challenges and continue learning.

Questions I get asked often.

A few questions that often come up about my process, my work, and how I approach projects.

What is my design process?

My process starts with understanding the problem, the users, and the context. I move from research and insights into ideation, wireframing, and prototyping - continuously iterating based on feedback. I see design as an ongoing cycle rather than a linear process.

What does a M.Sc. Media Informatics include?

My studies combine Computer Science with Human-Centered Design, covering areas such as Human–Computer Interaction, UX/UI Design, and Media Design, as well as Software Development, IT Security, AI and IT Ethics. This allows me to bridge the gap between design and development.

What job am I currently looking for?

I’m looking for a junior role in UX/UI, Web/App or Product Design. I’m particularly interested in working on complex or user-critical systems, building meaningful user experiences.

What makes me stand out from other designers and developers?

I combine UX thinking, visual design, and a solid understanding of frontend development. This allows me to create solutions that are not only user-centered and visually engaging, but also technically feasible and ready to be implemented in real-world products.
